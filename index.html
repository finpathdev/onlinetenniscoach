📹 For the most accurate analysis, film like this:
  • Camera angle: side-on to the court (standing at the net post or fence, facing the baseline), not from directly behind the baseline.
  • Camera position: mount on a tripod or steady surface — handheld footage makes ball tracking much less reliable.
  • Distance: far enough back that a full court line (baseline or singles sideline) is visible in frame — you'll use it to calibrate speed.
  • Lighting: film in good daylight or well-lit conditions; avoid heavy shadows across the court.
  • Clip length: upload short clips (a single rally or a few shots) rather than a full match — keeps analysis fast and accurate.
  • Background: avoid courts with yellow-green surfaces or backgrounds that closely match the ball color, since this can confuse ball tracking.

    How this works v0.3 prototype

    Pose tracking runs via MediaPipe. Ball tracking uses color + motion detection tuned for standard yellow-green tennis balls (works best with good lighting and a ball that contrasts against the court/background). Speed is converted to real mph using the calibration step, where you mark a known court distance once per video. Without calibration, speed falls back to a rough relative estimate. This is a lightweight CV approach, not a trained ball-detection model — it can lose the ball on fast blurred frames or busy backgrounds, and accuracy improves a lot with a clear side-on camera angle.
